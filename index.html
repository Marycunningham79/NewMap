Coastal States Adopt Electric Vehicles, But Registrations Lag Elsewhere

The United States have seen a huge jump in electric vehicles overall, but not all states are taking action.

By: Mary Cunningham

December 20, 2022

Electric vehicle registrations have risen significantly – more than 250% over the last five years – but it’s not necessarily a priority for all states.

Data reveals that west and east coast states have significantly higher registrations per capita than inland states. California, which announced that it would require new vehicles sold in the state to be zero emissions by 2035, is leading the charge. The west coast state ranked number one in terms of electric car registrations, boasting 39% of registrations nationwide, according to data obtained from the Alternative Fuels Data Center.

Despite its efforts to prioritize sustainability, New York ranks number 23. But stakeholders like the New York State Department of Environmental Conservation (DEC) are intent on reaching Governor Hochul’s clean transportation goals.

“New York State agencies and authorities will seek federal funding provided through the federal Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act to promote development of medium and heavy-duty ZEV infrastructure in New York,” said a DEC representative.
